A LOVE STORY FOR THE BIG SCREEN

The Story

ACT I — The Meeting

SCENE 1 — THE COFFEE SHOP

It began on an unseasonably cold autumn morning. Sarah was tucked into the corner booth of a tiny bookstore café, lost in the pages of a worn paperback. Michael walked in looking for an escape from the rain—and found something far more compelling. Two lattes, a shared umbrella, and three hours later, a story was born.

Neither of them knew it then, but that chance collision of timing and weather would rewrite the script of their lives. The barista later confessed she had never seen two people talk so long without checking their phones.

ACT II — The Courtship

SCENE 2 — THE YEAR OF YES

What followed was a montage of sun-drenched weekends, spontaneous road trips, and late-night conversations that stretched until dawn. They explored hidden bookshops, climbed mountains they were not prepared for, and built a world that belonged only to the two of them.

Every photograph from that year told the same story: two people who had found in each other something they did not know they were looking for. The world blurred around them; only the frame mattered.

ACT III — The Proposal

SCENE 3 — THE FINAL FRAME

On the same cliff where they had watched their first sunset together, Michael dropped to one knee. The sky was painted in shades of amber and rose—as if the universe itself had designed the perfect backdrop for the question that would change everything.

Sarah said yes before he could finish asking. The waves crashed below them in applause. It was the final scene of the first act, and the beginning of their greatest production yet.
